# Ubuntu Insurance Claims Analysis | SQL + PowerBI
# DataForge Africa Project
SQL and Power BI analytics project exploring insurance claims, fraud trends, and customer risk insights.

## 1. Project Overview

This project was completed as part of a short Data Analytics course and simulates a real-world insurance analytics environment. The objective was to analyze synthetic insurance data, identify trends, and build an interactive reporting solution using SQL and Power BI.

The dataset was intentionally designed with common data quality issues to reflect realistic business scenarios and support practical learning in data cleaning, transformation, and analysis.

## 2. Project Objectives

The main objectives of this project were to:

- Analyze insurance claims trends
- Identify fraud-risk patterns
- Explore customer behavior and demographics
- Develop interactive dashboards for business reporting
- Generate actionable business insights and recommendations

## 3. Tools & Technologies

- SQL (Databricks Free Edition) for data extraction, cleaning, and transformation
- Power BI for dashboard development and data visualization
- Github for project documentation

## 4. Dataset

The dataset includes: 
 Claim_Amount
 Claim_Date
 Province
 Age
 Policy_Type
 Premium_Amount
 Fraud_Flag

## 5. Key Findings

Total claims processed amounted to approximately R11.43 million.
Approved claims totaled approximately R7.48 million.
Female customers recorded slightly higher total claim values than male customers.
Customers aged 35–45 represented the highest-risk demographic segment.
Premium values remained relatively stable over time.

Limitations

The dataset only included customers who submitted claims and did not represent the insurer’s full customer base. Therefore, profitability metrics such as loss ratios could not be fully calculated.

## 6. Business Insights

Health insurance drives the highest claim frequency.
Life insurance drives the highest claim value.
Home and life insurance policies show the highest fraud exposure.
KwaZulu-Natal is the highest-risk province for claims and fraud activity.
Customers aged 35–45 represent the highest-risk demographic group.
Claims activity remained stable with limited seasonality observed.

## 7. Recommendations

- Strengthen fraud detection processes within home and life insurance products.
- Introduce enhanced claims verification controls in KwaZulu-Natal, particularly following disaster-related events.
- Apply closer monitoring and risk-based pricing strategies to the 35–45 age demographic segment.

The claims analysis dashboard focused on overall claims performance between January 2024 and December 2025.

Key Findings
Ubuntu Insurance processed 492 claims from 492 customers.
Approximately 67.5% of claims were approved.
Health insurance generated the highest number of claims.
Life insurance recorded the highest total claim value at approximately R4.46 million.
Property insurance recorded the lowest claim frequency and value.
Claims activity remained relatively stable over the two-year period with no major seasonal trends.
Business Insight

Health insurance drives claim frequency, while life insurance contributes the greatest financial exposure.
